Pitch Notes — Thursday

Not a script. Hit the bolded idea, say it your way, show the demo fast. The demo does the talking.

The opener (any shop)

"Hi, are you the owner? I'm Nick, I live here in town and I build websites for local businesses. I actually already built one for your shop — takes ten seconds to look at. Want to see it?"

Then hand them your phone with THEIR demo open. Don't explain first, show first. After they look: "If you like it, it can be live this week. Anything on it you don't like, I change — colors, photos, wording, prices, all of it. You approve it before you pay anything."

Why they need it (pick what fits)

  • Google is the new Main Street. When students and new residents search "barber near me" or "nail salon Moscow," businesses with a website show up first. Right now that's their competitors.
  • Facebook hides them. People without Facebook accounts hit a login wall. A website works for everyone, and they own it.
  • The placeholder problem. Some of them have auto-generated directory pages they don't control and probably don't know exist. That's what customers currently find.
  • Tap-to-call and directions are built in. Every demo has working call and map buttons — that's a booking or a walk-in from any phone.

The five stops

Angle: 30 years of reputation, zero web presence. New students arrive every fall and pick whoever they can find online. Big one: with short, unusual hours, a website that SHOWS those hours saves customers wasted trips and saves him the "are you open?" calls.

Objections you'll hear

  • "We have Facebook." Great, keep it — the site links to it. But Facebook buries posts and blocks non-users. The website is for everyone else, and it shows up on Google.
  • "How much?" Decide your number BEFORE you walk in and say it without flinching. One price to build, small yearly cost for the web address. One new customer a month pays for it.
  • "I need to think about it." Totally fine — text them the demo link on the spot so it's on their phone, get their number, follow up in 2–3 days.
  • "My nephew can do it." "He might! But this one's already done — you've seen it. If you like it, it's live this week."

If you close one

  • Get: their exact hours, prices they want shown, 5–10 photos, and how they want to be contacted.
  • Domain name runs about $10–15/year (like kazumisbarber.com) — that's a real cost, be upfront about it.
  • Get a deposit or first payment before you do custom work. Handshake plus cash is how this goes sideways.

Rules for the day

  • Owner or nobody. If the owner's out, ask when they're in and come back. Staff can't buy.
  • Show, don't lecture. Phone in their hands within 30 seconds.
  • Two noes and you're out gracefully — leave the link, stay friendly. It's a small town; today's no is next month's yes.
